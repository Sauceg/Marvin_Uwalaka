Bio

Marvin Uwalaka is a dedicated community organizer at Sencelosia, where he leads initiatives that bring local artists and residents together through community art workshops and events. His passion for community engagement extends to his role as Event Coordinator for the University of Alberta Black Student Association (U.A.B.S.A), where he is responsible for organizing and promoting events that align with the association’s mission.

Growing up in Nigeria and now residing in Edmonton, Alberta, Marvin is committed to uplifting communities. He was recognized by the Canadian Service Corps with a Certificate of Recognition for his valuable contributions to improving Canadian communities. Marvin has also played a key role in the Black Youth Leadership Program at the Center for the Advancement of African Canadians, where he empowers Black youth to lead community-based initiatives, fostering personal and communal development.

A first-class standing student, Marvin is currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science at the University of Alberta. With a CGPA of 3.5, his academic focus includes data structures, algorithms, and practical programming methodology. Marvin is passionate about using his technical skills and community leadership to make a lasting impact.
